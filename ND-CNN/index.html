Newcastle disease (ND) is a highly contagious and often severe disease found worldwide that affects birds including domestic poultry. It is caused by virulent strains of avian paramyxovirus type 1. The disease appears in three forms: lentogenic or mild, mesogenic or moderate and velogenic or very virulent, also called Newcastle disease. The lentogenic strains are very widespread but cause few disease outbreaks. It usually presents as a respiratory disease, but depression, nervous manifestations, or diarrhoea may be the predominant clinical form. Newcastle disease, in its highly pathogenic form, is a WOAH listed disease and must be reported. ND can present a clinical picture very similar to avian influenza, so laboratory testing is important to
confirm the diagnosis.

Source: https://www.woah.org/en/disease/newcastle-disease/

Monitoring and surveillance programs are crucial for early detection of Newcastle Disease outbreaks. These programs involve regular testing of birds, reporting any suspicious symptoms, and implementing quarantine and eradication measures in case of an outbreak.

To control Newcastle Disease, various measures are implemented, including vaccination, biosecurity practices, and surveillance programs. Vaccination is an essential tool in preventing the disease, especially in areas where the virus is endemic or highly prevalent. Additionally, strict biosecurity protocols, such as controlling access to farms, proper sanitation, and preventing contact with wild birds, help reduce the risk of disease introduction and spread.
